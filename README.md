
# Business Process Engineering (BPE) - Exercise Analysis
This repository contains an analysis of key business processes, focusing on their lifecycle, actors, and optimization strategies.

# 1. Order-to-Cash Process (Pharmacy Scenario)
This exercise analyzes the process of a customer ordering medicine and completing the payment.


# Process Type: 
Order-to-Cash (O2C). It covers the entire journey from the moment a customer submits a prescription to the final payment settlement.


# Key Actors: 
Includes the Doctor, Customer/Patient, Pharmacist, Technician, and Insurance Company.


# Core Tasks: 
The process involves receiving, checking, processing, filling, and delivering prescriptions.


# Value Proposition: 
The primary value is providing correct and safe medicines to ensure proper patient treatment.


# Performance Metrics: 
Success is measured by waiting time, accuracy of fulfillment, and overall customer satisfaction.


# Optimization Strategy: 
To address delays and manual errors, the process should be improved by automating the system and enhancing coordination between the pharmacist and insurance providers.

# 2. Procure-to-Pay Process (Corporate Procurement)
This exercise focuses on how a company acquires goods and pays its vendors.


# Process Type: 
Procure-to-Pay (P2P). It initiates when an employee needs a product and ends when the vendor is paid.


# Actors & Customers: 
The Actors are the Purchasing Department, Supervisors, and Vendors, while the Customers are the employees who receive the goods.


# Core Tasks: 
The cycle includes requesting, approving, ordering, receiving, and paying for goods.


# Key Outcomes: 
A request can result in being approved, rejected, delayed, or goods being returned.


# Performance Metrics: 
Efficiency is tracked through approval cycle time, delivery speed, and payment processing time.


# Proposed Changes: 
To eliminate "lost forms" and manual bottlenecks, the recommendation is to integrate digital systems and automate the approval workflow.

# Key Insight: BPR vs. Continuous Improvement
According to the exercise, Process Monitoring and Optimization are often considered outside the immediate scope of a Business Process Reengineering (BPR) project. This is because BPR focuses on fundamental redesign and radical change rather than incremental, continuous improvements.
